# Support Email QA Rubric

## 1. Tone and Professionalism

Checks whether the response sounds respectful, calm and customer-friendly.

**High quality response:**
- Polite and professional
- Avoids blame
- Uses clear and natural language

**Low quality response:**
- Cold or dismissive
- Sounds robotic
- Uses unclear or careless language

## 2. Empathy

Checks whether the response acknowledges the customer's frustration or concern.

**High quality response:**
- Recognizes the issue
- Shows understanding
- Uses a reassuring tone

**Low quality response:**
- Ignores the customer's frustration
- Gives a generic answer
- Sounds indifferent

## 3. Accuracy

Checks whether the information provided is correct and does not mislead the customer.

**High quality response:**
- Gives correct information
- Avoids unsupported claims
- Does not overpromise

**Low quality response:**
- Gives vague or wrong information
- Makes assumptions
- Promises something that cannot be guaranteed

## 4. Resolution Clarity

Checks whether the customer understands what will happen next.

**High quality response:**
- Explains the solution or next step
- Gives a clear timeline when possible
- Makes the action easy to understand

**Low quality response:**
- Leaves the customer confused
- Does not explain next steps
- Pushes responsibility back to the customer without help

## 5. Escalation Handling

Checks whether the response identifies when an issue should be escalated.

**High quality response:**
- Escalates billing, technical or account issues when needed
- Explains escalation clearly
- Sets realistic expectations

**Low quality response:**
- Ignores serious issues
- Does not escalate when needed
- Gives a generic answer to a complex problem
